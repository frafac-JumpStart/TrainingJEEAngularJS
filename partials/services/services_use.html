• Inject it as a parameter in the dependent object (controller, service, directive)...
  • ... And Angular dependency injection system will take care of the rest.
  • It will handle service instantiation and dependencies resolution for you.
  • Warning: The array notation allows to identify services in case you minify your js files, it's a good practice to use it
  • You can also use the nodejs plugin ng-annotate, which will add dependency injection annotations for you.
angular.module('myModule',[]) .controller('myController', ['$scope', 'myService', function($scope, myService){ $scope.myVar= myService.doJob(); }]) v
